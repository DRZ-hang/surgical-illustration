# Atlas Knowledge Guardrails (Text-Only, No Copying)

This guide encodes common, non-proprietary anatomical/pathology/surgical knowledge inspired by
standard atlases (e.g., Gray's, Netter, major pathology/surgical references). It is for
correctness only. Do NOT copy, trace, or replicate any atlas plates, compositions, palettes,
or labeling layouts. No image-to-image.

## Core Orientation & Naming
- Always use patient perspective for left/right.
- Keep anterior/posterior and superior/inferior consistent with the chosen view.
- Use standard anatomical nomenclature (Gray's/Netter conventions).
- If a segment system applies (e.g., Couinaud liver segments), follow accepted numbering.

## Global Anatomy Rules
- Preserve organ proportions and adjacency (lobes, fissures, ligaments, hilum positions).
- Major vessels must follow realistic branching planes (arteries, veins, ducts, nerves).
- If a view is specified (anterior/posterior/oblique/cross-section), landmarks must match.

## System-Specific Accuracy (High-Level)
- Hepatobiliary: portal triad relationships; portal vs hepatic veins; lobar/segmental layout.
- Cardiac: chamber orientation, valve positions, coronary course, great vessel continuity.
- Neuro: brain lobes, ventricles, brainstem/spinal cord continuity; avoid crossed laterality.
- Pulmonary: bronchial tree vs pulmonary artery/vein paths; lobar/segmental boundaries.
- GI: stomach/duodenum/colon continuity; mesenteric root and major vessels in plausible planes.
- GU: kidney-ureter-bladder continuity; renal hilum order; prostate/uterus position if shown.
- MSK: bone landmarks, joint axes, and muscle line-of-action must be plausible.

## Pathology Depiction (Schematic, Not Photorealistic)
- Use schematic patterns: lesion boundary, edema, necrosis, fibrosis, hemorrhage, thrombus.
- Generalize morphology for teaching; avoid patient-specific imaging look.
- Avoid gore; use restrained, didactic color coding.
- Show pathology in correct layer/compartment (mucosa vs muscle vs capsule vs parenchyma).

## Surgical Procedure Plausibility
- Sequence logic: exposure -> dissection -> control -> resection -> reconstruction/closure.
- Incisions and instrument approach should avoid impossible trajectories.
- Emphasize risk structures and safe planes; show landmarks for orientation.
- Reconstruction should connect correct tissue ends and vessel calibers.

## Labeling & Didactic Clarity
- Label only key structures; avoid dense, overlapping leaders.
- Use consistent terminology for arteries/veins/ducts/nerves and segment names.
- If showing steps, number panels and arrows consistently.

## Copyright & Similarity Rules
- Do NOT mimic any single atlas plate composition or style.
- Synthesize a new layout guided by the selected template/style only.
- Accuracy must be preserved regardless of stylistic constraints.
